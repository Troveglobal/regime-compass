Market Geometry

What shape is the market in?

Topology-family measures computed daily across the full 18-market board (crypto excluded). The TDA gauge (persistent homology) asks whether the geometry of market dynamics is deforming — a pattern that has preceded major drawdowns. The market tree (minimum spanning tree) shows the network skeleton of cross-market correlation — and when it collapses toward a single hub, history says pay attention. Both shipped with the audit that earned them a place.

The audit that earned this page

We don't add indicators because they're fashionable — every tool has to beat the incumbents somewhere, out loud. The original audit ran on 9 markets; when the board expanded to 20 in July 2026, the whole study was re-run on the full 18-market panel (crypto excluded) against the site's existing fragility gauges, Turbulence and the Absorption Ratio. Trailing percentiles only, no look-ahead, results published win or lose.

Does it add anything the incumbents don't have?

Two signals survive. The TDA gauge strengthened on the wider panel (overlap with turbulence fell to 0.15; hot readings preceded ~75% worse drawdowns). Hub concentration is the re-audit's discovery: on the original 9-market panel it looked inverted and we said so — with 18 nodes the star-collapse becomes statistically meaningful (76 hot-while-calm days across ~12 distinct episodes, ~2× worse forward drawdowns). We publish both versions of that finding because the reversal is exactly the kind of thing small panels do. Tree tightness remains structure, not signal — its information largely overlaps the absorption ratio (rank correlation 0.62).

Honest limits

A handful of episodes is still a small sample. Parameters follow the literature (60-day windows per Gidea & Katz; 90-day correlations per the MST literature) with no optimisation. These are environment measures: they say the market's geometry is deforming, not that a crash is scheduled. Treat readings as a prompt to check the regime board and stress gauges, never as a standalone signal.

Methodology

TDA landscape norm. Each day we take the trailing 60 daily return vectors of the 18 markets — a cloud of 60 points in 18-dimensional space — and compute its Vietoris–Rips persistent homology (H1: loops). The persistence landscape's L1 norm summarises how much robust geometric structure the cloud contains. Markets are scaled once by full-sample volatility so no single asset dominates; the day-to-day volatility structure is deliberately preserved — it is part of the signal. Smoothed over 10 days; the dial shows the trailing 5-year percentile. After Gidea & Katz (2018), "Topological data analysis of financial time series: Landscapes of crashes."

Market tree. From the trailing 90-day correlation matrix we build Mantegna's distance d = √(2(1−ρ)) and extract the minimum spanning tree — the shortest network connecting all 18 markets. Its mean edge length contracts when correlations rise; its shape shows which markets carry the connections. After Mantegna (1999) and Onnela et al. (2003), the workhorse of correlation-network analysis in financial-stability research.

Crypto is excluded from this page: 24/7 trading calendars distort the joint geometry of a daily-close panel.
