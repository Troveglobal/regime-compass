Nature of this tool

Regime Compass displays the output of statistical models — a 3-state Gaussian Hidden Markov Model, simple moving averages, and exponential moving averages — that classify each trading day for selected market indices into one of several regime states (bear / neutral / bull, or bull / bear depending on the model). The outputs are descriptions of recent observed market behaviour and not predictions of future prices.

It is a regime indicator, not a signal generator, forecast, or recommendation. The probabilities and regime labels shown have no predictive power over future prices. They describe what the model believes the market is, not what it will do.

Model limitations

  • Models lag regime changes by approximately 1–3 trading days on average. By the time an indicator confirms a regime change, the change has already begun.
  • The models are descriptive, not predictive. They estimate the current regime based on recent observations; they do not forecast future prices or regime changes.
  • Past performance does not guarantee future results. Historical regime classifications, however accurate, do not imply that the models will perform similarly in future market conditions.
  • The models can be wrong. Statistical models always carry uncertainty. Black-swan events, structural breaks, regime shifts, geopolitical surprises, and other unprecedented conditions may not be reflected.
  • Limited feature set. Information not encoded in the model inputs (price, volatility, FX moves, implied volatility where available) is invisible to the model. Anything else — fundamentals, flows, sentiment, news, macro releases — is not considered.
  • No transaction costs. Backtest figures are pre-cost gross returns. Real-world execution costs (commissions, taxes, slippage, bid-ask, financing) would reduce strategy returns and may make displayed performance unattainable in practice.
